Understanding Vascular Care

Vascular care focuses on diagnosing and treating diseases of the vascular system, which includes arteries, veins, and lymphatic circulation. These vital vessels are responsible for transporting blood and oxygen throughout the body. Any disruption in their function can lead to serious health issues including stroke, aneurysms, blood clots, or chronic venous insufficiency.

At One Stop Vascular, our mission is to ensure early diagnosis, minimally invasive treatment, and long-term management of vascular conditions for a better quality of life.

Types of Vascular Conditions Treated

At our vascular care centre in Hyderabad we specialize in the diagnosis and treatment of a wide array of vascular conditions including but not limited to :-

  • Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD) - A condition where narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to limbs causing leg pain during walking. Often linked to smoking diabetes and high cholesterol.

  • Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) - Formation of a blood clot in a deep vein usually in the leg. Can cause swelling pain and may lead to serious complications like pulmonary embolism.

  • Varicose Veins - Swollen twisted veins visible under the skin usually in the legs. Caused by weakened vein valves and often lead to discomfort, heaviness and aching.

  • Aneurysms - Bulging or weakening of a blood vessel wall commonly in the aorta. If ruptured it can cause life threatening internal bleeding and requires urgent treatment.

  • Carotid Artery Disease - Narrowing of carotid arteries in the neck due to plaque buildup reducing brain blood flow and increasing stroke risk. Often silent until a serious event.

  • Venous Ulcers - Chronic wounds usually near the ankles caused by poor blood circulation in leg veins. Healing is slow and requires vascular evaluation and long-term management.

  • Lymphedema - Swelling in arms or legs due to blocked or damaged lymphatic vessels. Often occurs after cancer treatment and can lead to discomfort, infections and skin changes.

  • Diabetic Foot Complications - Ulcers infections and poor healing in diabetic patients due to nerve damage and poor circulation. Requires prompt vascular care to prevent amputation and complications.

  • Vascular Malformations - Abnormal clusters of blood vessels present from birth. Can affect arteries veins or capillaries and may lead to pain swelling or bleeding if untreated.

  • Thoracic Outlet Syndrome - Compression of nerves or blood vessels between the neck and shoulder. Causes pain, numbness and weakness in the arms often worsened by certain arm positions.

Symptoms Indicating the Need for Vascular Care

Recognizing early signs of vascular issues is critical. If you experience any of the following it may be time to consult a vascular specialist :-

  • Leg pain while walking (claudication)

  • Swelling in the limbs

  • Visible varicose veins

  • Non-healing wounds or ulcers

  • Numbness or tingling in extremities

  • Discoloration in hands or feet

  • Cold feet or hands

  • Sudden vision problems or difficulty speaking (possible signs of stroke)


Who Needs Vascular Treatment?

Vascular diseases can affect people across all age groups but are more common among individuals with certain risk factors :-

  • Diabetes

  • Hypertension (high blood pressure)

  • High cholesterol

  • Obesity

  • Smoking history

  • Family history of vascular disease

  • Sedentary lifestyle

  • Pregnancy (for conditions like varicose veins)

Regular vascular check ups are recommended for people with any of the above risk factors.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q1: What is the most common vascular problem?

Varicose veins are the most common, especially among people who stand for long periods.

Q2: Are vascular treatments painful?

Most are minimally invasive with little pain, often done under local anesthesia.

Q3: How long is the recovery after vascular surgery?

It varies, but many resume daily activities within a few days.

Q4: Can vascular diseases be prevented?

Healthy habits like quitting smoking, controlling diabetes, and regular exercise can help prevent them.

Q5: How do I know if I need to see a vascular specialist?

Symptoms like leg pain, swelling, visible veins, or non-healing wounds should prompt a consultation.
